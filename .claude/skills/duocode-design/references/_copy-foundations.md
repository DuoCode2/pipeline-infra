# Copywriting Foundations

Rules governing all text content across DuoCode landing pages, for all 4 supported languages.

---

## Language-Specific Tone & Style

### English (EN)

- **Tone:** Warm, confident, approachable. Not salesy, not corporate.
- **Voice model:** "Family recipes, made fresh daily" — human, specific, grounded.
- **Avoid:** Superlatives without proof ("best in Malaysia"), generic claims ("world-class service"), buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "solutions").
- **Prefer:** Concrete details ("Serving Ipoh since 2003"), sensory language ("Crispy roti canai, made to order"), direct address ("You'll find us on Jalan Ampang").
- **Sentence length:** Mix short punchy sentences with one longer descriptive sentence per section.
- **Contractions:** Use them. "We're" not "We are". "You'll" not "You will".

### Bahasa Malaysia (MS)

- **Tone:** Mesra (friendly), yakin (confident), natural spoken BM.
- **Critical rule:** Write natural BM, never translate word-for-word from English. BM has its own sentence rhythm and idioms.
- **Keep original names:** Menu items, service names, and business names stay in their original language. Do not translate "Nasi Lemak" to anything — it is already BM.
- **Formal vs. informal:** Use semi-formal register. "Anda" (formal you) for professional services, "kami" (we) for warmth. Avoid overly casual "korang" or "kita" unless the brand voice is explicitly casual.
- **Common Malaysian terms:** Use "cawangan" (branch), "waktu operasi" (operating hours), "hubungi kami" (contact us), "tempahan" (booking/reservation).
- **Avoid:** Direct translations of English idioms, anglicisms where BM words exist ("servis" is acceptable, "solutionz" is not).

### Simplified Chinese (zh-CN)

- **Tone:** Concise, action-oriented, trustworthy.
- **Character set:** Simplified characters only. No traditional character mixing.
- **Food/product names:** Bilingual format — original name + Chinese translation. Example: "Nasi Lemak 椰浆饭", "Roti Canai 印度煎饼".
- **CTA style:** Direct and brief. "立即预约" (Book Now), "查看菜单" (View Menu), "联系我们" (Contact Us).
- **Number formatting:** Use Arabic numerals, not Chinese numerals, for prices and quantities. "RM12.90" not "十二令吉九十仙".
- **Punctuation:** Fullwidth Chinese punctuation throughout. Period is "。" not ".".

### Traditional Chinese (zh-TW)

- **Tone:** Same clarity as zh-CN but with traditional characters. Slightly softer tone compared to mainland style.
- **Character set:** Traditional characters only. "預約" not "预约", "價格" not "价格".
- **Hong Kong/Taiwan nuances:** Prefer Hong Kong Cantonese-influenced terms for food context when the business is Chinese-Malaysian. Use "訂位" (reserve seat) over "預訂" when contextually appropriate.
- **Food/product names:** Same bilingual format as zh-CN — original + traditional Chinese.
- **CTA style:** "立即預約", "查看菜單", "聯繫我們".

## CTA Frameworks

### Hero Section — PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

1. **Problem:** Identify a relatable pain point or desire. "Looking for authentic Penang laksa in KL?"
2. **Agitate:** Briefly amplify the need. "Most places just don't get the broth right."
3. **Solve:** Present the business as the answer. "We've been perfecting ours since 1985."
4. **CTA button:** Direct action verb. "View Our Menu", "Book a Table", "WhatsApp Us".

### Button CTAs — Direct Action

- Always start with a verb: "Book", "Order", "Call", "View", "Get".
- Maximum 4 words on a button.
- Primary CTA: filled button with `primary` color.
- Secondary CTA: outlined or ghost button with `accent` color.
- WhatsApp CTA: green (#25D366) with WhatsApp icon, always available as a floating button on mobile.

### Section CTAs

- Each major section should have one contextual CTA.
- Menu section: "View Full Menu" or "Order on WhatsApp".
- Services section: "Book This Service" or "Ask About Pricing".
- Reviews section: "Join Our Happy Customers".

## SEO Title & Meta Rules

### Title Tag Pattern
```
{Business Name} | {Industry Descriptor} in {City}
```
Examples:
- "Restoran Ah Kow | Seafood Restaurant in Petaling Jaya"
- "Salon De Belle | Hair & Beauty Salon in Bangsar"
- "Klinik Famili Jaya | Family Clinic in Shah Alam"

### Meta Description
- 150–160 characters maximum.
- Include city name and one key differentiator.
- End with a soft CTA: "Visit us today" or "Book your appointment".

### Open Graph
- `og:title` matches page title.
- `og:description` matches meta description.
- `og:image` should be the hero image or business logo.
- `og:locale` set to match page language: `en_MY`, `ms_MY`, `zh_CN`, `zh_TW`.

## Price Formatting

- **Always "RM" prefix.** Never "MYR", "Malaysian Ringgit", or "$".
- **No space** between "RM" and the number: `RM12.90` not `RM 12.90`.
- **Two decimal places** for prices with sen: `RM12.90`.
- **No decimals** for round prices in casual context: `RM13` is acceptable in display, but `RM13.00` in menus.
- **Price ranges:** Use en-dash: `RM15–RM25`.
- **"From" pricing:** `From RM29.90` or `Dari RM29.90` (BM).
